Treatment Overview

Total knee replacement is a surgical procedure in which parts of the knee joint are replaced with artificial parts (prostheses). It is performed on patients suffering from severe knee damage, typically caused by arthritis.

If you are suffering with arthritis, our specialized healthcare will offer suitable solutions to improve life quality through surgical options, including total knee replacement if required. The goal is to reduce pain and improve mobility.

Surgery involves removing damaged cartilage and bone from the knee and replacing them with an artificial joint. It's recommended for those with severe knee pain or stiffness that limits everyday activities and reduces quality of life.

Total knee replacement surgery might be required in conditions like osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, post-traumatic arthritis, or knee injury or deformity.

Eligibility For Treatment

Your eligibility for total knee replacement will be based on your medical condition and various other factors. While it's a common procedure for individuals over 50, younger patients may also be candidates based on specific conditions.

Typical criteria healthcare providers consider include:

  • Age: While there's no specific age limit, most patients are over 50
  • Pain level: Moderate to severe chronic pain that limits daily activities
  • Non-response to conservative treatments: Failure to respond to medications, physical therapy, etc.
  • Any existing health conditions: that might impact surgeries like diabetes, heart disease

Preparation Before Treatment

Proper preparation before knee replacement surgery is crucial for successful outcomes and smoother recovery. Your healthcare provider will guide you through all necessary steps and requirements.

Common preparation guidelines include:

  • Medical evaluation: Comprehensive assessment including physical examination and tests
  • Medication review: Adjustment or temporary discontinuation of certain medications
  • Home preparation: Making necessary modifications to ensure safe post-surgery recovery
  • Pre-surgery exercises: Strengthening knee muscles to support faster recovery

Post-Treatment Care Treatment

Proper post-surgery care is essential for optimal recovery and long-term success. Although knee replacement is a major surgery, most patients experience significant improvement in pain and mobility following the procedure.

Important post-treatment care guidelines include:

  • Rehabilitation: Prescribed physical therapy regimen
  • Pain management: Following medication schedule as directed
  • Activity restrictions: Gradually increasing mobility while avoiding certain movements
  • Wound care: Proper cleaning and dressing of the surgical site

Treatment FAQs

How do I know the pain I am experiencing is arthritis?

Arthritic pain typically includes joint stiffness, swelling, reduced range of motion, and a grating sensation. A medical professional can diagnose arthritis through physical examination, imaging tests, and sometimes lab tests.

I need a knee replacement but can't take time off work before I decide when I can have surgery?

Discuss with your healthcare provider about minimally invasive options, partial knee replacement, or scheduling during a less busy work period. Some patients can return to desk jobs within 2-4 weeks, though full recovery takes longer.

Can I see Doctor before I decide and what about the post surgery?

Yes, a consultation with the surgeon is standard practice before scheduling surgery. Post-surgery, you'll have follow-up appointments to monitor your recovery progress and address any concerns.
